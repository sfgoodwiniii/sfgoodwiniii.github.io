Fishnet Algorithm Fluid Droplet Physics Simulation
Rochester Institute of Technology
May 2022 --- Aug 2022

During the summer of 2022, I worked as a computational physics researcher who was optimizing and expanding on the functionality of a previous graduate student. The simulation would find the minimal surface for an evaporating liquid droplet on different roughness and interfaces between them.

I used C/C++ and the Fishnet Algorithm to find minimal surfaces using numerical integration of pressure. The final results were then plotted by MATLAB for animations and surfaces. In the process, I documented my work in LaTeX using Overleaf so that my research could be continued after me.
